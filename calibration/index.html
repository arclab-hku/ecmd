Calibration



1. Time synchronization


We use a Precision Time Protocol (PTP) device to synchronize the clocks of various data collection devices across the sensor network. The PTP ensures time accuracy within nanoseconds. The synchronization device acquires the NMEA output and pulse-per-second (PPS) signal from a u-blox M8T GNSS receiver to align the ROS time of the onboard computers with the GPS time. This enables sensors such as cameras, LIDAR and IMU to record timestamps based on the synchronized GPS time.


2. Sensors Calibration


For sensor calibration, we first define the coordinate of Xsens Mti-30 as the body frame. Then we meticulously calibrate the intrinsics of each sensor with the extrinsics between the external IMU and the rest sensors. More details of the calibration sequence, platform layout, and reports of calibration can be found on our website.


2.1. Calibration of Inertial Measurement Unit


To calibrate the IMU, we position it on a level surface for a duration of three hours, recording the raw measurements. Utilizing the Kalibr toolbox, we can accurately calibrate the random walk and Gaussian white noise of IMU.


2.2. Calibration of Standard Cameras


For the calibration of standard cameras, we move the sensor platform against the 9×7 checkerboard in XYZ-RPY-axis and collect the sequence of images and IMU. Then intrinsics and extrinsics calibration of standard cameras are achieved by Kalibr toolbox, where the pinhole and radial-tangential camera models are adopted. The results of calibration are shown in Fig. x.


2.3. Calibration of Event Cameras


For the calibration of event cameras, DAVIS346 can produce fix-rated frames, enabling image-based calibration, while DVXplorer merely produces asynchronous event streams. Therefore, E2VID is used to achieve image reconstruction from event streams. Using the reconstructed checkerboard images, the intrinsics and extrinsics of event cameras can be calibrated following the same procedure as standard cameras.


2.4. Calibration of Infrared Cameras


Since infrared cameras solely capture the temperature rather than the intensity difference, we design a distinct checkerboard to make the pattern detectable for both intensity and infrared cameras. As shown in Fig. x, the checkerboard intervals are affixed with aluminum materials, then using a heating plate to raise the temperature of the calibration board. Since the superior thermal dissipation of aluminum compared to plastic, a temperature contrast emerges between the two materials, enabling infrared cameras to distinctly capture the lattice shape of the checkerboard. With the same approach as standard cameras, the calibration of intrinsics and extrinsics of infrared cameras can be achieved.


2.5. Calibration of mechanical LiDAR


Precise temporal offset and extrinsics between LiDAR and IMU are crucial to LiDAR-Inertial Odometry. For the calibration of mechanical LiDAR, LI-Init is capable of achieving temporal and spatial calibration for LiDAR and IMU without checkerboards or extra devices. We rotate and move the device around XYZ-axis to ensure sufficient excitation until the data accumulation is completed, thus we acquire the extrinsic transformation between LiDAR and IMU.
